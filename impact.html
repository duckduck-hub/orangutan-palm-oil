How Palm Oil Harms Orangutans

Orangutans are critically endangered species due to habitat loss caused by the palm oil industry. The expansion of palm oil plantations leads to the destruction of their natural habitat, displacing these animals and putting their survival at risk.

Deforestation for palm oil not only affects orangutans directly by destroying their homes but also reduces the biodiversity of the ecosystem, which can lead to further loss of species that share their habitat.

Moreover, the fragmentation of forests affects the orangutans’ ability to find food and reproduce, leading to a dramatic decline in their populations. Conservation efforts are essential to protect these magnificent creatures and their environment.
